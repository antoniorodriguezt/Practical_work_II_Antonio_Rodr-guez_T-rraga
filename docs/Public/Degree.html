Business Analytics and Computer Engineering

What does the business analytics and computer engineering degree consist of?

A degree that combines Business Analytics and Computer Engineering, integrating technical, analytical, or business skills to prepare students for roles that integrate technology skills, such as software development, multiple science knowledge, or computer systems and hardware. However, decision making is also crucial for business analytics careers, since this is based in marketing, Big Data, and predictive analytics. This career offers skills to be productive in the labor market, including computing skills like programming with software tools, system architecture design, or data modeling. You can also acquire analytical thinking from business careers, provided by marketing and Big Data knowledge, and develop your perspicacity in business, understanding organizational needs... There are also plenty of career opportunities, as this bachelor equips students with analytical and informatics skills that are crucial in the labor market. Some of the principal roles in enterprises could be data scientists, who create complex algorithms and machine learning techniques to analyze data and make predictions. Another career opportunity could be a software engineer, who develops applications, operating systems… This requires programming skills in languages such as C++, Java, Python. From the business side, we can highlight the role of business intelligence analysts, who collect and analyze business data to predict trends and create reports that assist in financial decision making in an enterprise.

Computer Engineering

Computer engineering is a path of engineering careers which includes multiple paths related to technology and computing, like development of hardware systems and software, design… This field integrates principles of electrical engineering and computer science to create computer hardware and software systems. It also encompasses sectors such as electromagnetism, artificial intelligence (AI), robotics, computer networks, computer architecture, or operating systems. Computer engineering also involves the creation of computer components such as microcontrollers, microprocessors, development of personal computers, supercomputers, or the design of electrical circuits based on robotics. From the software sector, you can acquire knowledge about programming to develop programs, apps, and websites using languages such as C++, HTML, JavaScript, among others.

Business Analytics

Business analytics is concerned with the development and application of quantitative approaches to support marginal decision-making. It consists in analyzing data and building statistical models. The Business Analytics bachelor focuses on understanding the contexts of decision-making and combining this with a deep knowledge of data analytics techniques involving statistics and Big Data. This bachelor offers the opportunity to learn skills such as predictive analytics, machine learning, business acumen, and problem-solving and decision-making.

Subjects

Year 1:

Basics of Computer Engineering, Economy, Business, Law, Government, University Studies, Critical Thinking, and Skills. Introduction to Data Science, Big Data, and Technology for Business. Mathematics and Engineering courses including Programming, Electronics, and Technology.

Year 2:

Microeconomics, Statistics, and Probability. Humanities: History of Science, Anthropology, Education for Social Responsibility. Data Governance, Big Data, Databases, and Analytics. Mathematics, Discrete Math, and Programming.

Year 3:

Advanced Data Modeling, Visualization, Machine Learning, Big Data Technologies, and Decision Support Systems. Business courses like Marketing, Tax System, Finance, and Technological Entrepreneurship. Cloud Computing and Data Analytics, with Computer Science subjects like Algorithms and System Architecture.

Year 4:

Data and Sustainability, Natural Language Processing, People Analytics, and Financial Analytics. Ethics in the Digital Era and Complementary Training. Computer Engineering topics: Software Engineering, Web Development, AI, and Networks.

Year 5:

Datathon, Business Intelligence, Business Strategy, and Marketing Analytics. Final Degree Projects, External Internships, Software Engineering, HCI, Security, and IT Project Management.

Year 6:

Quality Management and High Performance Computing. Final Degree Project.
